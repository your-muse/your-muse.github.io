LITERARY

Cancer

My fingers close in on nothing. Open again. Flex. Repeat. The pale pink drapes obstruct the afternoon sun from completely lighting up the room. It's a comfortable agony. A brutal serenity.

I fist the comforter, pulling it higher. It does nothing to hide me from the inevitable reality.

I breathe in. “I make you sad?” I breathe out.

He sighs. Exasperated. Fed up.
“I'm sadder without you.”

A treacherous finger comes to caress my naked arm.

My attempts to focus on the squeaky ceiling fan are futile. The pillow under my head absorbs my sniffle.
“That's not how it's supposed to be.”

My hand travels up his chest, halting at the nape of his neck.

He wouldn't look at me. Perhaps ashamed. Perhaps something else. I have the urge to sit in that corner of this room where the sun can't reach.

“That's how it is, I'm afraid.” The audible resignation twists the knife deeper.

I cough once.
Twice.

There is a pool of blood.

He runs to get a towel. Probably.
I would never know.
